About the Grand Canyon

The Grand Canyon, located in northern Arizona, is one of the most iconic geological landmarks in the world. Formed over nearly two billion years of Earth's history and carved by the Colorado River over millions of years, the canyon stretches approximately 277 miles in length, reaches widths of up to 18 miles, and descends over a mile deep. Its vast scale reveals layered bands of red rock, each representing different geological eras, making it a living record of Earth's past.

Explore Activities

Hiking Trails

The Bright Angel and South Kaibab trails are among the most famous routes, offering stunning views and access deep into the canyon. These trails range from moderate to extremely challenging, with dramatic elevation changes.

Helicopter Tours

Helicopter tours provide a unique aerial perspective, allowing visitors to witness the immense scale of the canyon, the winding Colorado River, and hidden rock formations not visible from the rim.

Colorado River Rafting

Rafting trips along the Colorado River take visitors through the canyon itself, combining thrilling rapids with breathtaking scenery and geological exploration.
